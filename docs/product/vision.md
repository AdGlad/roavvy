# Product Vision

## The Idea in One Sentence

Roavvy turns the photos already on your phone into the most complete, private record of everywhere you've ever been.

---

## The Problem

Travellers accumulate thousands of photos but no easy way to see the full picture of where they've been — or to relive how they got there. Travel tracking apps exist, but they all ask for something: manual check-ins, continuous location access, or uploading your photos to a server. The friction is too high, and for privacy-conscious users, the trade-offs are unacceptable.

Meanwhile, every photo taken with a smartphone contains the answer — GPS metadata attached to the moment it was taken.

---

## The Solution

Roavvy reads the GPS metadata from your existing photo library, resolves it to countries, regions, and cities entirely on your device, and turns that data into a rich, explorable record of your travel life. Your trips, your countries, your continents — all discovered automatically. No manual logging. No continuous tracking. No photos uploaded.

The scan takes minutes. The map is permanent. The stories are yours.

---

## What the Experience Feels Like

You open Roavvy for the first time. Within minutes of scanning, a world map fills in with every country you've ever visited. You tap France and see that you've been there four times — Paris in 2015, Provence in 2018, the Alps in 2021, Nice in 2023. You see the photos from each trip, the regions you explored, the cities you passed through. You had forgotten about Provence.

You share your travel card. You unlock "Continental Drift" for the third continent. You order a poster of your map for the wall.

---

## Who It's For

**Primary:** Frequent travellers (5+ countries visited) who are proud of where they've been and want to see it visualised, explored, and shared.

**Secondary:** Casual travellers who want a simple, private way to remember where they've been without the overhead of a travel journal or dedicated tracking app.

**Not for:** People who want real-time itinerary planning, hotel/flight booking, or social travel networks.

---

## Core Values

**Private by design.** Photos never leave your device. This is not a policy promise — it is how the app is built. Users should feel safe giving it access to their photo library.

**Zero effort.** The user's job is to take photos and live their life. Roavvy does the rest. No check-ins, no tagging, no manual logging required.

**Rich without being overwhelming.** The map is the anchor. Depth (trips, regions, cities) is revealed progressively as the user explores — not thrown at them all at once.

**Honest.** If we can't detect a visit automatically, we say so. We don't pad the map. Users can add trips and countries manually, but the app doesn't incentivise gaming the count.

**Ownable.** The travel map, the achievements, and the sharing cards should feel like something the user created — not a dashboard generated by an algorithm.

---

## What Success Looks Like

- A user scans their photo library and says "I forgot I'd been there."
- A user taps a country and relives a trip through the photos from that week.
- A user shares a travel card without hesitation because they know their photos weren't uploaded.
- A user buys a personalised travel poster because the map is worth putting on the wall.
- A user shows a friend their stats screen — 47 countries, 6 continents, 112 trips — and the friend downloads the app.

---

## Geographic Resolution Levels

Roavvy detects at four levels of geographic granularity:

| Level | Granularity | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Continent | 6 inhabited | Europe, Asia |
| Country | ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 | France (FR), Japan (JP) |
| Region | ISO 3166-2 admin1 | Île-de-France, Provence |
| City | Major settlement (~100k+) | Paris, Marseille |

All detection is offline, on-device, using bundled geodata. No network call is ever made to resolve a location.

---

## Out of Scope (Intentionally)

- Real-time location tracking.
- Social feed or following other users.
- Travel recommendations or itinerary planning.
- Android (iOS-first; Android is a future consideration post App Store launch).
