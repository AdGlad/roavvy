# Product Vision

## The Idea in One Sentence

Roavvy turns the photos already on your phone into a complete, private record of everywhere you've been.

---

## The Problem

Travellers accumulate thousands of photos but no easy way to see the full picture of where they've been. Travel tracking apps exist, but they all ask for something: manual check-ins, continuous location access, or uploading your photos to a server. The friction is too high, and for privacy-conscious users, the trade-offs are unacceptable.

Meanwhile, every photo taken with a smartphone contains the answer — GPS metadata attached to the moment it was taken.

---

## The Solution

Roavvy reads the GPS metadata from your existing photo library, resolves it to country codes entirely on your device, and presents the result as a world map of everywhere you've been. No manual logging. No continuous tracking. No photos uploaded. The scan takes minutes; the map is permanent.

---

## Who It's For

**Primary:** Frequent travellers (5+ countries visited) who are proud of where they've been and want to see it visualised — and share it.

**Secondary:** Casual travellers who want a simple, private way to remember where they've been without the overhead of a travel journal or dedicated tracking app.

**Not for (v1):** People who want real-time itinerary planning, hotel/flight booking, or social travel networks.

---

## Core Values

**Private by design.** Photos never leave your device. This is not a policy promise — it is how the app is built. Users should feel safe giving it access to their photo library.

**Zero effort.** The user's job is to take photos and live their life. Roavvy does the rest. No check-ins, no tagging, no manual logging required.

**Honest.** If we can't detect a visit automatically, we say so. We don't pad the map. Users can add countries manually, but the app doesn't incentivise gaming the count.

**Ownable.** The travel map, the achievements, and the sharing cards should feel like something the user created — not a dashboard generated by an algorithm.

---

## What Success Looks Like

- A user scans their photo library, sees their travel map, and says "I forgot I'd been there."
- A user shares their travel card without hesitation because they know their photos weren't uploaded.
- A user buys a personalised travel poster because the map is worth putting on the wall.

---

## Out of Scope (Intentionally)

- Real-time location tracking.
- Social feed or following other users.
- Travel recommendations or itinerary planning.
- City or region-level detection (country-level only, at least in v1).
- Android (iOS-first; Android is a future consideration).
